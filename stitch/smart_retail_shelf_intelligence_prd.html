Problem Statement 2: Smart Retail Shelf Intelligence: Computer Vision-Driven Inventory Monitoring and Demand Optimization Description: Retail out-of-stock events cost the global industry an estimated $1 trillion annually in lost sales. Despite heavy investments in ERP and supply chain systems, the last-mile visibility gap remains: retailers often do not know the real-time state of their shelves until a customer complains or a manual audit is performed. Meanwhile, overstocking ties up capital and leads to markdowns and waste, especially for perishable goods. Existing camera infrastructure in stores is vastly underutilized, primarily serving only security purposes. Your challenge is to build an intelligent shelf monitoring and inventory optimization system that leverages computer vision on existing store camera feeds combined with demand forecasting to provide real-time shelf-level visibility, planogram compliance checking, and automated replenishment recommendations. Challenge Requirements: 1. Shelf Image Analysis and Product Detection: Use computer vision (YOLO, Faster R-CNN, or Vision Transformers) to analyze shelf images and detect: product presence/absence, stock levels (full, low, empty), planogram compliance (correct product placement), and price tag accuracy. The model should handle varying lighting conditions, camera angles, product orientations, and partial occlusions from customers. Implement SKU-level product recognition to distinguish between similar-looking products on the same shelf. 2. Automated Planogram Compliance: Build a planogram comparison engine that takes the intended shelf layout (provided as a structured JSON or image reference) and compares it against the detected shelf state from camera feeds. Identify misplaced products, missing facings, incorrect price tags, and unauthorized products. Generate compliance scores per aisle and per shelf section. 3. Demand Forecasting and Replenishment: Build a time-series forecasting model that predicts product-level demand at each store location using historical POS data, promotional calendars, weather data, and local event schedules. The forecast should drive automated reorder point calculations and generate replenishment orders with suggested quantities and timing to minimize both stockouts and overstock. 4. Real-Time Alert System: Implement an alerting pipeline that notifies store associates within 5 minutes of detecting a stockout or planogram violation. Alerts should be prioritized by revenue impact and include suggested corrective actions. Support multiple notification channels (dashboard push notifications, mobile alerts, and email digests). 5. Dashboard and Analytics: Create a management dashboard showing shelf health scores, stockout frequency heatmaps by aisle and time of day, planogram compliance trends, demand forecast accuracy metrics, and estimated revenue recovered through faster replenishment. Include a visual shelf map that overlays detection results on store floor plans. Technology Requirements: Python (PyTorch/TensorFlow for CV models, scikit-learn/Prophet for forecasting), OpenCV for image processing, PostgreSQL or MongoDB for storage, a message queue (Redis or RabbitMQ) for alert delivery, and a visualization layer (Streamlit/Dash/React).
